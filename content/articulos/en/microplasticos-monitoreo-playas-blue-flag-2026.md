The Foundation for Environmental Education (FEE) confirmed that, starting with the 2026 season, municipalities applying for or renewing Blue Flag certification will need to report on the presence of microplastics on their beaches, a criterion added alongside the existing water-quality and waste-management standards.

## Why this criterion was added

Recent studies cited by the organization show a growing presence of microplastics in coastal sediments worldwide, even on beaches with good bathing water quality under traditional parameters. The measure aims to have certified municipalities adopt sampling protocols and, where possible, plans to reduce single-use plastics in the coastal environment.

## Industry reaction

Marine science specialists consulted by trade media described the update as a necessary step to keep the label relevant in light of new scientific evidence, though they warned that implementation will depend on local laboratories' capacity to carry out the required analysis, especially in countries with fewer technical resources.
