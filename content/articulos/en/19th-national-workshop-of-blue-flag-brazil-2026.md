## National Gathering Strengthens Blue Flag Standards

From April 16 to 19, 2026, the municipality of Porto de Pedras, in Alagoas, welcomed the 19th National Workshop of the Blue Flag Program Brazil. The gathering brought together government representatives, environmental specialists, and program coordinators from across the country to exchange experiences and evaluate the progress of sustainable beach and marina management nationwide. Discussions centered on strengthening the credibility of the certification, aligning technical guidelines, and reviewing successful case studies from sites such as Lagoa do Peri, Prainha, and Praia das Pedras de Itaúna.

## Field Visits Highlight Certification in Practice

A key component of the workshop was a series of technical visits designed to illustrate how Blue Flag criteria translate into everyday coastal management. Patacho Beach, the only Blue Flag-certified site in Alagoas, offered participants a look at five consecutive seasons of sustained compliance, underscoring the long-term commitment required to maintain the award. Attendees also toured Lage Beach, currently undergoing a pilot evaluation phase, gaining insight into both the achievements and remaining challenges on the path toward certification. A visit to the Peixe-Boi Association, recognized nationally for its manatee conservation work, further emphasized the link between biodiversity protection and coastal tourism management.

## Expanding the Program's Reach

The event opened with a regional meeting aimed at municipalities and organizations considering joining the Blue Flag network, outlining certification criteria, benefits, and the steps required to enter the pilot phase. Institutional partners also contributed to the discussion: the Secretariat of the Union's Heritage presented tools such as Beach Management Adjustment Agreements and the Orla Project, while the Brazilian Lifesaving Society shared drowning-prevention data and launched, in collaboration with Blue Flag, a new award recognizing best safety practices at beaches and marinas. Coordinators from the Green Key and Eco-Schools programs, both under the Foundation for Environmental Education, also took part, highlighting the broader network of environmental certifications operating in Brazil.

## Local Culture and a Shared Commitment to Sustainability

Beyond technical sessions, the workshop featured performances by local cultural groups, reinforcing regional identity alongside the environmental agenda. Officials noted that hosting the event positions Porto de Pedras as a reference point for coastal sustainability efforts in Brazil, reflecting continued investment in environmental management and quality of life for coastal communities. Organizers described the workshop as a foundation for planning and monitoring efforts throughout the 2026 certification cycle, reinforcing a growing network of destinations committed to responsible coastal management.

The event was organized by Instituto Ambientes em Rede, the entity responsible for coordinating the Blue Flag Program in Brazil, in partnership with the Municipality of Porto de Pedras, the Costa dos Corais Convention & Visitors Bureau, and the Alagoas State Secretariat of Tourism.
