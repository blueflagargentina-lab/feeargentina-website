## A Sculpture Born From Community Effort
Balneario La Florida, a Blue Flag-certified beach in Argentina, has introduced a large-scale public artwork titled 'La Travesía del Sauce Criollo' (The Journey of the Creole Willow), designed to strengthen the city's environmental education programme. The initiative forms part of the broader Blue Flag framework, which requires participating beaches to maintain active efforts in waste management, water quality monitoring, safety standards and public environmental awareness.

## Art Inspired by Nature
The sculpture was created by Alejandro Propato, an artist recognized internationally for his large-scale works installed in natural public spaces. The piece draws inspiration from the Creole willow, a tree commonly found along riverbanks whose seeds are naturally dispersed by water. While the trunk and branches of the sculpture recreate the tree's structure, its foliage is being built collectively using strips of reused PET plastic, transforming a common waste material into a symbol of environmental renewal.

## Community Participation Across the City
More than 30 local institutions have joined the project, including cultural centers, non-governmental organizations, neighborhood associations, university faculties, municipal offices, the local aquarium and a river advisory council. Twenty-five schools are also taking part by helping construct the sculpture's foliage. To support the effort, nine plastic bottle collection points have been set up throughout the city, located at municipal district offices, cultural centers, an urban arts school, a humanities faculty and a public library.

## Looking Ahead to 2026
Organizers say the project goes beyond artistic expression, aiming to encourage public reflection on consumption habits and their environmental impact, while promoting reforestation with native species. City representatives noted that uniting community participation is central to generating lasting change. As Blue Flag beaches worldwide prepare for the 2026 certification cycle, initiatives like this one in Argentina highlight how art and public engagement can reinforce coastal sustainability efforts at the local level.
