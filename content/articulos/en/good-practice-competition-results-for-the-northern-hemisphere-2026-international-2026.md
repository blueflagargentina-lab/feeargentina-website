## A Global Showcase of Sustainable Coastal Practices

As part of the Northern Hemisphere 2026 International Evaluation, Blue Flag International called on its National Operators to present outstanding sustainability initiatives from their respective countries. This year's competition centered on two key themes: strengthening collaboration among FEE programmes and promoting circular economy models within coastal communities. The initiative reflects the organization's ongoing effort to encourage knowledge-sharing and innovation across its global network of certified beaches and marinas.

## Strong Participation Across Continents

The 2026 edition drew significant engagement, with ten countries submitting a total of 22 Good Practice entries. Nine projects focused on collaboration between FEE programmes, while thirteen highlighted circular economy solutions. An international jury convened on April 22, 2026, to evaluate the submissions based on criteria such as relevance, creativity, adaptability, collaboration potential and replicability in other coastal regions.

## Winners in the FEE Collaboration Category

Spain's Mijas Municipality claimed first place with its "Community Tree Planting and Coastal Conservation" initiative, which mobilizes local schools and volunteers to restore native Mediterranean vegetation near Blue Flag beaches, including El Chaparral. Denmark's Friluftsrådet earned second place for its marine education resources developed jointly with Eco-Schools and local nature guides, while Mexico's Los Cabos Municipality took third place with its hydroponic and aquaponic learning gardens designed to teach students about sustainable food systems.

## Leading Circular Economy Initiatives

In the circular economy category, Japan's Yanmar Sunset Marina secured first place for transforming invasive aquatic plants from Lake Biwa into compost used to grow cotton for staff uniforms. Spain's Yaiza Municipality placed second with a school-based program combining composting and environmental education, while Türkiye's Fethiye Municipality and Croatia's Camp Kovačine shared third place for initiatives promoting responsible waste management among boaters and campers.

## Recognizing Innovation Across the Network

Blue Flag International congratulated all participants, emphasizing that these Good Practices serve as valuable inspiration for coastal communities worldwide. By highlighting successful, replicable models, the competition reinforces the Blue Flag certification's broader mission to advance environmental education and sustainable management of beaches and marinas globally.
