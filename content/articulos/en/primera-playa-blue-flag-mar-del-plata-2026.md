A beach on Mar del Plata's northern coast received Blue Flag certification in May 2026, becoming the first coastal site in Argentina to join the international programme run by the Foundation for Environmental Education (FEE), operated locally under the name Bandera Azul by FEE Argentina.

## A years-long process

The certification is the result of joint work between the local municipality, lifeguards and environmental organizations, including regular water-quality monitoring, the installation of segregated waste bins, and environmental education campaigns aimed at beachgoers during the summer season.

## What it means for the region

Mar del Plata's addition to the world map of Blue Flag sites places the South Atlantic on a programme historically dominated by the Mediterranean and northern Europe. FEE Argentina noted the certification must be renewed each season and said other beaches along the Buenos Aires coastline are already being evaluated for 2027.
