Several coastal municipalities in the western Mediterranean announced in 2026 the expansion of their Posidonia oceanica restoration programmes, an endemic seagrass that acts as a carbon sink and protects the coastline from erosion — factors increasingly valued in the environmental assessment of Blue Flag beaches.

## An ecosystem under pressure

Posidonia meadows have receded in various parts of the Mediterranean due to indiscriminate boat anchoring and rising water temperatures. Environmental organizations working with certified municipalities have been promoting eco-friendly mooring buoys, which prevent anchors from damaging the underwater meadows.

## Link to Blue Flag certification

While Posidonia is not currently a mandatory programme criterion, the Foundation for Environmental Education looks favorably on nearby marine ecosystem conservation plans when assessing a municipality's overall environmental management. Specialists consulted anticipate that seagrass meadow health could become a more formal certification indicator in future editions of the programme.
