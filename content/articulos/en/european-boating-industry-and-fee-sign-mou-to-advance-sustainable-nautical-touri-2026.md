## A New Alliance for Sustainable Boating

European Boating Industry (EBI), which represents the continent's recreational boating sector, has formalised a partnership with the Foundation for Environmental Education (FEE), the organisation responsible for the Blue Flag eco-label. The agreement, signed through a Memorandum of Understanding, aims to accelerate sustainable practices across European nautical tourism, combining policy influence with internationally recognised environmental standards.

## Combining Policy and Certification Expertise

The collaboration brings together two distinct strengths: EBI's direct engagement with European policymakers and industry stakeholders, spanning boat manufacturing, marinas, and tourism services, and FEE's long-standing leadership in environmental certification through programmes such as Blue Flag and Green Key. By pooling these capabilities, both organisations intend to create a stronger, unified voice advocating for responsible growth in the boating industry, while reinforcing shared environmental goals across coastal and marine destinations.

## Shared Commitments and Statements

Under the terms of the agreement, EBI and FEE will engage in ongoing cooperation that includes mutual promotion of each other's initiatives, joint development of projects tied to shared sustainability priorities, and regular information exchange. Philip Easthill, Secretary-General of EBI, described the partnership as a meaningful step for the recreational boating sector in Europe, highlighting the goal of balancing industry growth with environmental responsibility. Daniel Schaffer, CEO of FEE, said the collaboration reinforces the organisation's mission to protect marine and coastal environments while supporting innovation within the boating community.

## Looking Toward 2026

As the partnership moves into its implementation phase, both organisations expect the collaborative framework to shape joint initiatives and awareness campaigns ahead of the 2026 boating season. The alliance is positioned as a foundation for long-term cooperation, with the aim of embedding sustainability more deeply into marina operations, boatbuilding standards, and nautical tourism practices across Europe.
