## A Milestone Training Seminar in Ankara
The Foundation for Environmental Education in Türkiye (TÜRÇEV) held its 16th Local Blue Flag Responsibles Training Seminar from 24 to 26 November 2025 at the Conference Hall of the Ministry of Environment, Urbanization and Climate Change. The three-day event brought together senior officials and technical experts to strengthen the local management structure behind Türkiye's Blue Flag Programme, one of the country's most recognized coastal sustainability initiatives.

## High-Level Participation and Institutional Support
The seminar featured contributions from Fatih Turan, Director General of Environmental Management at the Ministry of Environment, Urbanization and Climate Change, and Dr. Abdulkadir Ateş, Chairperson of TÜRÇEV and former Minister of Tourism. Representatives from the Ministry of Culture and Tourism, the Ministry of Health, the Turkish Coast Guard Command and the Turkish Underwater Sports Federation also took part, underscoring the multi-sector coordination required to sustain Blue Flag standards nationwide.

## Certifying a New Generation of Local Responsibles
More than 150 participants, representing 54 municipalities, four tourism unions and NGOs, six companies and thirteen public institutions, successfully completed the training and received the Local Blue Flag Responsibles Training Certificate. These newly certified individuals will be authorized to implement and monitor Blue Flag criteria at the local level on behalf of their respective institutions. TÜRÇEV reports that, cumulatively, 1,731 municipal staff members have now completed this training since the program's inception.

## Türkiye's Growing Blue Flag Footprint
Since joining the Blue Flag Programme in 1993, Türkiye has steadily expanded its certified sites. As of 2025, the country holds Blue Flag status for 557 beaches, 29 marinas, 18 tourism boats and 8 individual yachts, placing it third globally among more than 50 participating nations. Local Blue Flag Responsibles, typically appointed by municipalities, NGOs, village service unions or accommodation facilities, coordinate day-to-day compliance work for both awarded and candidate sites in partnership with TÜRÇEV.

## Looking Ahead
With training programs like this one continuing on a regular basis, TÜRÇEV aims to further reinforce local capacity for environmental management as Türkiye prepares for its 2026 Blue Flag certification cycle, reaffirming its position as a leading force in global coastal and marine sustainability efforts.
