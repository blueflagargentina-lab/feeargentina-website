## A Legacy of Coastal Protection

Since 2001, Morocco has worked to safeguard and celebrate its extensive 3,500-kilometer coastline through the Lalla Hasnaa Sustainable Coastline Awards. Spearheaded by the Mohammed VI Foundation for Environmental Protection under the patronage of Her Royal Highness Princess Lalla Hasnaa, the initiative began as a modest "Clean Beaches" program and has since grown into a nationally recognized model for participatory coastal stewardship. The program's values closely mirror those championed by Blue Flag, the international FEE certification known for promoting environmental education, biodiversity conservation, and waste reduction along beaches and marinas worldwide.

## Community Innovation on Display

Held every two years, the awards invite municipalities, schools, NGOs, businesses, and private citizens to showcase original coastal conservation projects. Entries have ranged from playful fish-shaped recycling stations designed to engage the public, to art installations crafted from collected plastic waste. Other standout initiatives include accessible boardwalks that improve beach access for people with reduced mobility, as well as citizen-science efforts tracking microplastic pollution along the shore. An independent panel of marine scientists, sustainability specialists, and community representatives evaluates each submission based on its relevance, measurable results, originality, and potential for replication in other coastal regions.

## Growing Momentum and Global Recognition

Over four completed editions, the awards have drawn 440 applications and honored nearly 100 projects, reflecting rising public interest in environmental stewardship across Morocco. The upcoming fifth edition represents an important milestone, as it aligns with the priorities of UNESCO's Decade of Ocean Science for Sustainable Development, spanning 2021 to 2030. This connection, first established during the fourth edition in 2022, has embedded the awards within the UNESCO Ocean Decade framework, reinforcing their contribution to global sustainability goals including quality education, clean water access, sustainable communities, climate action, marine life protection, and cross-sector partnerships.

## Looking Ahead to 2026

As Morocco strengthens its position as a regional leader in sustainable coastal management, the fifth edition of the Lalla Hasnaa Sustainable Coastline Awards is expected to attract even greater participation heading into 2026, further embedding community-led conservation into the country's environmental strategy. Organizers hope the program will continue to serve as a replicable model for coastal governance, encouraging local action that resonates on a national and regional scale.
