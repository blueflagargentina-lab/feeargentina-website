## New Certifications Confirmed for 2025/2026

The Blue Flag International Jury held its second meeting of the year this past September, resulting in the certification of 21 new sites for the Southern Hemisphere 2025/2026 season. The newly awarded locations include beaches, marinas and tourism boats that met the programme's strict environmental, safety and accessibility criteria, joining the growing network of certified coastal destinations heading into 2026.

## A Growing Global Network

These additions complement the sites already awarded during the Northern Hemisphere evaluation round held in April 2025. Combined, the two rounds bring the total number of Blue Flag certified sites to 5,216 across 51 countries. The figure breaks down into 4,323 beaches, 738 marinas and 155 tourism boats, reflecting the continued expansion of the certification both in traditional strongholds and in newer participating regions.

## Best Practice Recognition

Alongside the new certifications, Blue Flag International also recognized the winners of its Best Practice competition for the Southern Hemisphere 2025/2026 evaluation cycle. The competition highlights sites that go beyond baseline requirements, showcasing innovative approaches to environmental management, waste reduction and community engagement at certified beaches and marinas. The full ranking of winning sites has been published by the organization.

## Partnerships Behind the Programme

Blue Flag International reiterated its gratitude to the institutional partners that support the certification process with technical expertise and guidance. These include the European Environment Agency, ENAT, EUCC, ICLEI, ICOMIA, the International Life Saving Federation, UNEP, UNESCO, UN Tourism, the World Sustainable Hospitality Alliance, CitiesWithNature, the Climate Service Center Germany (GERICS), the Caribbean Hotel & Tourism Association, the University of Aberdeen, IUCN and WCA. Their continued collaboration underpins the credibility of the Blue Flag standard as it moves into another certification cycle in 2026.
