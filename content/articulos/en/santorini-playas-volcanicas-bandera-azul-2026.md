Greece, the country with the second-most Blue Flag awards in the world, confirmed in 2026 the renewal of certification on several Santorini beaches, including the iconic volcanic-sand coasts of Perissa and Kamari. The news comes amid a broader regional debate on the limits of mass tourism across the Aegean islands.

## Managing visitor flow

According to the municipal authority, renewing the label required a stricter waste management plan and new environmental information points for visitors. Reduced-traffic time slots were also introduced in the most sensitive coastal sectors, a measure meant to balance tourist flow with the conservation of the coastal ecosystem.

## A model other islands are watching

Sustainable tourism specialists say Santorini's case could become a reference for other Greek islands seeking to certify their beaches without overwhelming environmental carrying capacity. The Blue Flag, awarded annually, requires independent audits verifying continuous compliance with standards throughout the entire 2026 season.
