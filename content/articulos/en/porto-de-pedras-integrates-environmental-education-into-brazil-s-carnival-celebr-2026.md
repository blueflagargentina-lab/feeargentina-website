## A Carnival with an Environmental Message
On 14 February 2026, the municipality of Porto de Pedras, located on Brazil's northeastern coast, held another edition of its now-traditional "Bloquinho Bandeira Azul," or Blue Flag Carnival Parade. The event combined the festive spirit of Brazil's Carnival season with messages about environmental protection and public health, showing how local traditions can be used as a platform for sustainability outreach.

## From the Green Tunnel to Patacho Beach
The parade began at the "Green Tunnel," a natural corridor formed by dense coastal vegetation, and concluded at Patacho Beach, which has held Blue Flag certification for four consecutive seasons. Organized through a partnership between the Municipality of Porto de Pedras, the Secretariat of Environment, and the Secretariat of Tourism, the initiative illustrates how coordinated efforts among local institutions can keep environmental awareness at the center of community celebrations.

## Health and Environment as a Shared Concern
This year's parade carried the theme "The harm done to the environment is felt by the body," linking issues such as pollution, improper waste disposal, and environmental contamination to their direct effects on public health and quality of life. Participants, both residents and visitors, were encouraged to consider how everyday choices affect not only coastal ecosystems but also community well-being.

## Sustainability Beyond Municipal Size
With a population of roughly 7,000 residents, Porto de Pedras illustrates that meaningful environmental commitment is not limited to large cities. Municipal Secretary of Tourism Lívia Crisiane noted that the parade allows the town to celebrate its cultural identity while reinforcing care for its beaches and the health of its people, describing it as a way to welcome visitors responsibly.

## A Broader Social Dimension
The 2026 edition also incorporated awareness activities led by the Municipal Secretariat for Women's Affairs, focused on preventing violence against women. This addition expanded the scope of the parade beyond environmental themes, positioning the Blue Flag Carnival Parade as a multifaceted community event that connects sustainable tourism practices with broader social well-being.
