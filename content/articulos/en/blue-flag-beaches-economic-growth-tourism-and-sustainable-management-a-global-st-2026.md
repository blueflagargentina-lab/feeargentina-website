## A Scholarly Look at Blue Flag's Global Reach

A new academic publication is offering an in-depth analysis of the Blue Flag programme, one of the world's most recognized eco-certification schemes for beaches and marinas. The book, titled *Blue Flag Beaches. Economic Growth, Tourism and Sustainable Management*, was formally presented on 10 June 2024 in Murcia, Spain, during a day-long series of lectures dedicated to the certification's history and evolution. Edited by researchers María A. Prats and Fernando Merino, the volume brings together contributions from economists, sustainability experts and tourism scholars from multiple countries.

## Examining Economic and Environmental Outcomes

The study traces the origins of Blue Flag as an early model for sustainability certification and reviews how it has been adapted across different regions. Drawing on case studies from five continents, the authors examine the political and managerial hurdles that national programmes have faced over the decades, while also highlighting how the certification has supported environmental education, capacity building and community involvement in coastal management. Particular attention is given to the programme's role in advancing the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals through local and regional initiatives.

## Spain's Long-Standing Role in the Programme

The presentation took place at the Faculty of Economics and Business of the University of Murcia, one of the academic institutions closely tied to Spain's early adoption of the Blue Flag scheme. Speakers at the event included representatives from local and regional authorities, academic researchers and long-time professionals associated with the certification, who discussed both the national trajectory of Blue Flag in Spain and its broader international significance. The session was livestreamed, allowing wider audiences to follow the discussions in real time.

## Looking Toward Future Challenges

Beyond documenting past achievements, the book also considers the future direction of the Blue Flag programme, outlining potential challenges and opportunities as environmental standards and tourism demands continue to evolve. With coastal destinations increasingly focused on sustainable development, the findings are expected to remain relevant well into 2026 and beyond, as governments and tourism operators seek data-driven approaches to balancing economic growth with environmental protection.
