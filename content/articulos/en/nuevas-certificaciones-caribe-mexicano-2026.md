Mexico announced in 2026 the addition of twelve new beaches to the Blue Flag programme in the state of Quintana Roo, mainly in the municipalities of Solidaridad and Tulum. With this expansion, the country reinforces its position as one of Latin America's leading Blue Flag destinations, a programme that assesses water quality, waste management and coastal safety.

## Requirements met

State authorities detailed that the process included installing wastewater treatment plants near the coast and training local tourism cooperatives in solid-waste management, two of the central pillars required for the certification's annual renewal.

## Tourism and conservation

The region's hotel sector welcomed the news as a differentiator against other Caribbean beach destinations, while local environmental organizations called for the growth in certifications to be accompanied by clear limits on coastal construction so as not to compromise nearby mangrove ecosystems.
