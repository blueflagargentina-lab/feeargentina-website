## A Global Platform for Sustainable Development

Between 17 and 22 May 2026, Azerbaijan hosted the 13th session of the World Urban Forum (WUF 13), one of the leading international gatherings focused on sustainable urban development. Among the sessions selected by UN-Habitat through a competitive process was a proposal from the EkoSfera Ecology-Social Center, which centered on sustainability standards and their practical implementation at the local level.

## Introducing a National Sustainability Standard

The EkoSfera-led session served as a platform to present Azerbaijan's Sustainable Practices Standard (SPS), described as the country's first national framework aimed at embedding sustainability principles across multiple sectors and regions. The initiative reflects a broader effort to translate international best practices into locally relevant tools for environmental management.

## Blue Flag Recognized as a Benchmark Programme

During discussions on certification systems and sustainability benchmarks, Dilara Hajiyeva, Blue Flag National Coordinator for Azerbaijan, spoke about the value of the Blue Flag Programme as a globally recognized environmental certification. She noted that the programme demonstrates how international standards can strengthen environmental protection, improve site management, support environmental education, and foster sustainable tourism. Alongside Blue Flag, attendees also learned about other Foundation for Environmental Education (FEE) initiatives, including Green Key and Eco-Schools, both presented as accessible tools for tourism operators, schools, and communities seeking to adopt sustainable practices.

## Building Momentum for Blue Flag in Azerbaijan

Speakers underscored the importance of pairing globally recognized certifications with nationally adapted frameworks to advance progress toward the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). For Azerbaijan, the timing was particularly significant: the country recently secured approval of its Blue Flag feasibility study, positioning WUF 13 as a key opportunity to raise awareness among urban planners, environmental experts, and policymakers. The forum is expected to support the next stages of Blue Flag's rollout in the country as national authorities work toward certification in the coming years.
