## A Caribbean Hub for Sustainable Tourism Dialogue

Tobago positioned itself as a regional leader in eco-conscious travel after hosting the 2024 Blue Flag National Operators Meeting (NOM) from October 15 to 18. Organized by Green T&T, the Tobago Tourism Agency Limited (TTAL), and the Foundation for Environmental Education (FEE), the gathering brought together more than 50 delegates from 30 countries to the Shaw Park Cultural Complex in Scarborough. Discussions centered on strengthening environmental standards for beaches, marinas, and boat tour operations under the internationally recognized Blue Flag certification framework.

## Aligning Local Action with Global Goals

The meeting's agenda was shaped around FEE's GAIA 20:30 strategy, which targets climate change, biodiversity loss, and pollution through coordinated global action. Sessions tackled these themes directly, featuring input from the Caribbean Climate-Smart Accelerator, the Trinidad and Tobago Meteorological Services, and conservation groups working on turtle protection and UNESCO Man and the Biosphere designations. Local secondary schools also presented youth-led environmental initiatives, reflecting a broader effort to embed sustainability education into the certification's long-term vision.

## Toward a Blue Economy Policy Framework

Beyond dialogue, the NOM served as a working session to draft a sustainability policy for boat tour operators, a initiative led by Green T&T and the Blue Flag International Office with backing from the UNDP Global Environment Facility Small Grants Programme. Delegates from the Tobago House of Assembly, private sector representatives, and civil society leaders reached preliminary consensus on measures to support the blue economy while aligning with national sustainability goals. Officials indicated the resulting framework is expected to guide certification standards well beyond the current cycle, with implementation milestones anticipated through 2026.

## Culture, Diplomacy and Environmental Heritage

The event opened with remarks from Chief Secretary Farley Augustine and EU Ambassador Peter Cavendish, who highlighted shared priorities between the European Union and Trinidad and Tobago in advancing sustainable tourism policy. Cavendish also referenced Tobago's Main Ridge Forest Reserve, protected under 1776 legislation considered among the earliest environmental laws of the modern era, as a symbol of the island's long-standing conservation ethos. Delegates later toured Blue Flag-certified sites, including Buccoo Reef and Fort King George, while a student-led fashion show made from recycled Blue Flag banners underscored creative approaches to sustainability messaging.

## Looking Ahead

Green T&T is now consolidating recommendations from the meeting to finalize the proposed boat tour operator policy and plans to release documentary content capturing the event's outcomes. As the national operator for the Blue Flag Programme in Trinidad and Tobago, the organization said the 2024 NOM reinforces the island's ambition to serve as a model for eco-friendly travel across the Caribbean in the years leading up to 2026.
