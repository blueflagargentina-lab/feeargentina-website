## New Blue Flag Sites Confirmed for 2026

Blue Flag International has released the results of its first International Jury meeting of 2026, held this past April, confirming a new group of beaches, marinas and tourism boats that will carry the Blue Flag label during the upcoming Northern Hemisphere season. The announcement marks the official start of the certification calendar for the year and reflects the programme's continued expansion into new destinations and countries.

## Global Figures for the 2026 Season

According to the organization, a total of 5,274 sites have been awarded the Blue Flag certification worldwide for 2026. The count includes 4,371 beaches, 745 marinas and 158 tourism boats, spread across 53 participating countries. These numbers highlight the programme's role as one of the most recognized eco-labels for coastal and marine tourism sites, evaluating criteria related to water quality, environmental management, safety standards and public education.

## Good Practice Competition and Institutional Partners

Alongside the certification announcement, Blue Flag International also congratulated the upcoming winners of its Good Practice competition, part of the Northern Hemisphere 2026 International Evaluation process. The final ranking of this competition, which recognizes exemplary initiatives among certified sites, is expected to be published in the coming week.

The organization further acknowledged the support of its network of institutional partners, which includes the European Environment Agency (EEA), UNESCO, UN Tourism, IUCN, ICLEI, ICOMIA, the International Life Saving Federation (ILS), the World Sustainable Hospitality Alliance, the Caribbean Hotel & Tourism Association, the University of Aberdeen, and several other environmental and tourism-focused bodies. These collaborations, Blue Flag noted, remain central to maintaining the credibility and technical rigor of the certification process each season.

## What This Means for Coastal Destinations

For local authorities, marina operators and tourism boards, the 2026 award cycle represents both recognition and responsibility, as certified sites must continue to meet strict annual criteria to retain their Blue Flag status. As the Northern Hemisphere season progresses, travelers and coastal communities alike are expected to see continued emphasis on sustainable practices, water quality monitoring and environmental education at Blue Flag sites around the world.
