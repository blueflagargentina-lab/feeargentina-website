## A Global Showcase of Coastal Innovation
As part of its Northern Hemisphere 2025 International Evaluation, Blue Flag International invited National Operators to submit Good Practice examples highlighting local efforts in environmental stewardship. This year's competition focused on two pressing themes: measures to prevent pollinator and insect loss, and initiatives promoting climate adaptation along coastlines.

## Strong Participation Across Continents
The 2025 edition drew significant international engagement, with 22 Good Practices submitted by 13 countries. Of these, 14 projects addressed climate adaptation strategies, while 8 focused on protecting pollinators and insects in coastal environments. An international jury convened on April 10, 2025, to evaluate the submissions based on relevance, creativity, adaptability, collaboration potential, and replicability across different regions.

## Climate Adaptation Winners
In the climate adaptation category, Denia, Spain, claimed first place for its environmental volunteering initiative at Punta del Raset beach, which mobilized 60 participants to restore dune ecosystems and protect nesting habitats for the Kentish plover. Mexico's Hotel Wyndham secured second place with its Coastal Green project, which planted 400 native species to combat erosion and boost biodiversity. Third place was shared between Denmark's Municipality of Ishøj, for its Beach SOS coastal resilience program, and Spain's Municipality of Teulada, for dune awareness workshops engaging local communities.

## Pollinator Protection Recognized
The pollinator and insect loss prevention category was led by Delray Beach, Florida, whose Pocket Parks and Pollinator Gardens initiative created ten biodiverse green spaces in collaboration with residents and wildlife authorities. Portugal's Municipality of Leiria took second place with its Pollinator Friendly Schools Network, which engaged students in habitat restoration across six schools. Spain's Municipality of Gavà rounded out the top three with its citizen science project, BIO Platges Met, which invites beachgoers to help monitor local biodiversity.

## Looking Ahead
Blue Flag International congratulated all participants, noting that these Good Practices play a vital role in strengthening the global Blue Flag network and inspiring further innovation in coastal management. As preparations begin for future evaluation cycles leading into 2026, organizers expect continued growth in sustainability initiatives across certified beaches, marinas, and tourism operators worldwide.
