## Guarujá Hosts National Celebration

The coastal city of Guarujá, in the state of São Paulo, welcomed Brazil's National Blue Flag Award Ceremony on October 31, an event that formally opened the 2025/2026 season for the country's certified beaches and marinas. Held at the historic Iate Clube de Santos, the gathering drew mayors, tourism and environmental officials, and representatives of conservation groups from across Brazil to mark the achievement of 60 destinations approved by national and international Blue Flag juries.

## Five States, Growing Recognition

This season's awarded sites span five Brazilian states. The Northeast region contributed six locations, with one in Alagoas and five in Bahia. In the Southeast, 23 destinations earned the distinction—19 in Rio de Janeiro and four in São Paulo. Santa Catarina once again led the national ranking with 31 certified sites, reinforcing its long-standing role as Brazil's top-performing state under the program.

## Local Pride and Long-Standing Commitment

Guarujá's Praia do Tombo drew particular attention, having secured its 16th consecutive Blue Flag season—the longest active streak in South America. Mayor Farid Madi called the recognition a source of civic pride and a reflection of the municipality's ongoing environmental commitments. Odoardo Lantieri, environmental manager at Iate Clube de Santos, echoed that sentiment, describing the award as both a mark of excellence and a shared responsibility among coastal communities. Leana Bernardi, national coordinator of the Blue Flag program in Brazil through Instituto Ambientes em Rede, emphasized that each flag represents sustained local effort involving public agencies, businesses, and residents working toward shared coastal conservation goals.

## Awards for Environmental Education and Innovation

Separate recognitions highlighted standout environmental education efforts. Bombinhas, in Santa Catarina, received the Environmental Education Recognition Award from the National Jury for the third year running, covering initiatives across five local beaches. Other notable projects included a ghost-fishing awareness campaign in Cabo Frio and a climate-change communication effort in Bombinhas. In the Marinas category, Iate Clube de Santa Catarina and Marina Kauai in Ubatuba were recognized for community and sustainability initiatives. At the international level, Brazil secured three Good Practice Competition awards, including a Southern Hemisphere win for the Iate Clube de Santa Catarina's oyster-farming and ocean-economy education program, along with two honors in the pollinator-protection category for projects in Iguaba Grande and Porto de Pedras.

## Program Standards and Next Steps

To maintain certification, beaches and marinas must meet annual criteria set by the Foundation for Environmental Education (FEE), covering water quality, environmental management, safety, education, and social responsibility. The ceremony was organized by Instituto Ambientes em Rede in partnership with the Municipality of Guarujá and Iate Clube de Santos, with support from Embratur and the Visite Guarujá Convention & Visitors Bureau. Official flag-raising across the newly certified sites began on November 1, coinciding with the start of the Southern Hemisphere summer season.
