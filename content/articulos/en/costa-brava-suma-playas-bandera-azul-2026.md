The Foundation for Environmental Education (FEE) confirmed in June 2026 the addition of five new Costa Brava beaches to the international list of Blue Flag certified sites, the eco-label that assesses water quality, safety, accessibility and environmental management at coastlines and marinas worldwide.

With this expansion, Spain consolidates its position as the country with the largest number of Blue Flag awards globally, a trend that has held for more than a decade thanks to sustained investment by coastal municipalities in sanitation infrastructure and water monitoring.

## Criteria assessed

The certification process — renewed every season — reviews more than 30 indicators grouped into four areas: environmental education and information, bathing water quality, environmental management of the surrounding area, and safety and services for visitors. Municipalities must also guarantee access for people with reduced mobility and provide visible recycling facilities to the public.

Local authorities in the Catalan region noted that renewing the certification required tightening discharge controls and expanding recycling points along the seafront, in line with the standards the programme requires for 2026.

## Impact on tourism

Local tour operators expect the news to boost hotel occupancy during the northern-hemisphere summer, since the Blue Flag acts as an internationally recognized mark of trust for travelers who prioritize destinations with sound environmental management.
