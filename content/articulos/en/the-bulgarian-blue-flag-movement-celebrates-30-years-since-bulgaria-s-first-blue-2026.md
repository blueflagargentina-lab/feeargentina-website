## A Symbolic Return to Where It All Began

On July 4, 2025, the Bulgarian Blue Flag Movement gathered at Sunny Beach to mark exactly 30 years since the country earned its first Blue Flag distinction. The commemorative event, held under the theme “30 Years of Clean Water, Trust and Safety,” recreated the original 1995 flag-raising ceremony down to the precise time of day, honoring the moment Sunny Beach became Bulgaria’s pioneering entry into the international Blue Flag program.

## Sea, Sky and Symbolism

As part of the tribute, a Blue Flag was carried out of the water by a boat towing a parachute, creating a visual link between the sea, the coastline and the human effort behind sustainable beach management. Kiril Spasov of the Association of Concessionaires of Seaside Beaches described the flag as carrying not only recognition but also “memories, responsibility, and meaning,” calling Sunny Beach a national model for sustainable coastal development rather than simply a tourist destination.

## Three Decades of Growth

Stanimir Georgiev, Chairman of the Bulgarian Blue Flag Movement, noted that Bulgaria now holds 30 Blue Flag awards, a milestone he attributed to consistent daily and seasonal efforts by beach operators across the country. In 2025, Bulgaria achieved a record number of certified beaches and marinas, yet Sunny Beach remains the symbolic starting point of the country’s Blue Flag journey, the location where Bulgaria first appeared on the global map of certified coastal sites.

## Rising Water Quality Standards

Alongside the anniversary celebrations, Bulgaria recorded another achievement in 2025: the European Union ranked the country second in Europe for bathing water quality, an improvement from fifth place the previous year. Officials credited this progress to beach managers who continue to apply Blue Flag criteria in their daily operations, reinforcing environmentally responsible practices. As Bulgaria looks toward the 2026 certification season, the anniversary serves as both a celebration of past achievements and a benchmark for maintaining high standards of cleanliness, safety and sustainability along its coastline.
