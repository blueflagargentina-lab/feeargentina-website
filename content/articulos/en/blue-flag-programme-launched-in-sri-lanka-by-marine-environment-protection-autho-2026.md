## A New Chapter for Sri Lanka's Coastline
Sri Lanka has formally entered the global Blue Flag network, following an official launch event organized by the Marine Environment Protection Authority (MEPA). Held in Colombo, the ceremony introduced the internationally recognized eco-label to the country's tourism and environmental sectors, marking a step toward stronger coastal management standards.

## Government and International Support
The launch drew senior officials from Sri Lanka's Ministry of Urban Development and Housing, along with representatives from the Ministry of Tourism and the British High Commission. MEPA's leadership described the certification as an opportunity to improve environmental practices along the coast while attracting eco-conscious travelers. Five pilot sites—including Mirissa, Arugam Bay, and Unawatuna—were named as the initial beaches working toward certification under a signed cooperation agreement.

## Guidance from the Global Network
A mentor appointed by the Foundation for Environmental Education, the organization overseeing Blue Flag worldwide, took part in the event to share lessons from similar programmes in the region, particularly India's experience integrating Blue Flag standards into coastal tourism. These insights are expected to help Sri Lankan authorities adapt the certification's environmental, safety, and accessibility criteria to local conditions as preparations continue toward broader implementation by 2026.

## Building Local Capacity
MEPA is working with several environmental organizations, including groups focused on ocean conservation, biodiversity restoration, and community outreach, to support the programme's rollout. Officials noted that a dedicated national operator will likely be needed to manage certification processes over time, ensuring pilot beaches meet Blue Flag requirements consistently. Alongside the launch, MEPA also coordinated a coastal clean-up involving local volunteers, reflecting early community engagement in the initiative.

## Looking Ahead
As Sri Lanka works to expand its participation in the Blue Flag programme, authorities hope the certification will support sustainable tourism growth while reinforcing protections for the country's coastal ecosystems in the years ahead.
