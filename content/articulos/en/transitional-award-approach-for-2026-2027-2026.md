## A Temporary Bridge Between Regulatory Frameworks

Blue Flag International, run by the Foundation for Environmental Education (FEE), has announced a transitional two-season award covering both 2026 and 2027. The move responds to shifting international certification standards, including ISO/IEC 17065:2012 and the EU's Directive (EU) 2024/825, and is intended to give the global network time to adjust without disrupting existing operations.

Officials stressed that the change is administrative rather than a reflection of programme performance. Beaches, marinas and boats that hold the Blue Flag in 2026 will keep their certification through the 2027 season, provided they continue to meet compliance rules, undergo annual monitoring, and pass a yearly review. A complete reapplication will not be required for already-certified sites in 2027, though National Juries will still carry out a focused evaluation each year, with final confirmation from the International Jury.

## What Changes and What Doesn't

Despite the extended award period, Blue Flag sites will keep operating on their usual seasonal calendar, and all existing criteria, oversight levels and fee structures remain untouched during the 2026–2027 window. The certification can still be withdrawn if a site fails to meet requirements, and in some cases award decisions may be issued pending final Bathing Water Quality (BWQ) data, with confirmation dependent on validated results. New applicants in 2027 will be limited to non-EU countries, following full evaluation, with their certification lasting until updated criteria are expected to take effect in the 2028 season.

## Preparing for a Revised Certification Model

According to FEE leadership, the transitional approach is designed to protect the credibility of the Blue Flag brand while regulatory processes in Europe are finalized. Johann Durand, Blue Flag International Director, said the mechanism allows the programme to "meet evolving regulatory expectations while maintaining the Programme's credibility, oversight, and global consistency." FEE CEO Daniel Schaffer described it as a "responsible and forward-looking step," while FEE President Nikos Petrou emphasized that education for sustainable development and strong governance remain central to the initiative.

## Looking Toward 2028

The two-year mechanism is meant to buy time for the development of an updated set of Blue Flag criteria, expected to roll out from 2028 onward. National Operators, National Juries and the International Jury will continue to be involved throughout the transition, and Blue Flag International says it will keep providing guidance and communication support to ensure the certification process remains consistent across all participating countries during this adjustment period.
