## Tackling a Growing Threat

Plastic waste remains one of the most pressing challenges facing coastal and marine environments, with single-use items accounting for more than half of the roughly 500 million tons of plastic produced annually worldwide. Much of this material is never recycled, eventually making its way into seas and oceans. Along the Mediterranean coast of Turkey, a collaborative environmental initiative has spent the 2024 summer season addressing this issue directly on Blue Flag-certified beaches in the Antalya region.

## A Multi-Year, Multi-Partner Initiative

The project, now known as "Plastic-Free Shores, Plastic-Free Waters," builds on a beach stewardship program first launched in 2010 by the Foundation for Environmental Education in Türkiye (TÜRÇEV). Reorganized in 2023 through a partnership between TÜRÇEV, the Ecological Research Society and the Antalya Metropolitan Municipality, the initiative gained additional backing this year, including business sector support from BETUYAB and international funding channeled through Conservation Collective, tied to a global music tour and a Swiss watchmaker's sponsorship.

## Recycling Innovation and On-Site Engagement

Recycling technology company TOMRA Collection expanded its involvement in 2024, showcasing a demonstration deposit return machine at public events to introduce beachgoers to advanced recycling methods. Additional participation came from organizations and brands including Star Diving Academy, Precious Plastics Turkey, Pebble, Sürdür Store and Zeero, alongside several Blue Flag-awarded hotels in the area.

## Summer Activities Across Certified Beaches

Field activities took place across a series of Blue Flag beaches throughout the season. The program began in late June at Konyaaltı Public Beach, continued in early July at TURAŞ Belek Public Beach, and included "Plastic-Free July" workshops for children at the Antalya Metropolitan Municipality's Environmental Education and Innovation Center, a site previously recognized with a Blue Flag International Best Practice Award. The final 2024 event was held in early September at TURAŞ Lara Public Beach, closing out a season of hands-on education and community involvement.

## Looking Toward Continued Impact

Organizers indicate the project will continue building momentum in the years ahead, with plans already forming for expanded activities heading into 2026 as part of a longer-term effort to reduce single-use plastic dependency along Mediterranean shores. Project leaders credited the volunteers, trainers and visiting citizens whose participation helped sustain the campaign's reach throughout the region's certified beaches.
