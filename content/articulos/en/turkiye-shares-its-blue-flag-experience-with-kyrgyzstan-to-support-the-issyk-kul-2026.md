## Kyrgyz Delegation Studies Türkiye's Blue Flag Model

A high-level delegation from Kyrgyzstan traveled to Türkiye to examine the practical implementation of the Blue Flag Programme, part of an ongoing initiative led by the Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ) to explore certification opportunities around Issyk-Kul Lake. The visit was coordinated under the guidance of the Foundation for Environmental Education (FEE), with support from FEE Türkiye, the country's national Blue Flag operator.

The delegation included representatives from the Tourism Development Agency under the Cabinet of Ministers of the Kyrgyz Republic, regional and local authorities from the Issyk-Kul district, members of the Kyrgyz Association of Tourism Organizations (KATO), the Association of Resorts of Kyrgyzstan (AKK), leaders from four pilot resorts, and GIZ advisors. Nurgazy Esengeldi uulu, First Deputy Head of the Issyk-Kul District Administration, headed the group.

## On-the-Ground Training in Antalya

Over a five-day program, participants visited several certified sites, including Konyaaltı Public Beaches under Antalya Metropolitan Municipality, beaches managed by Muratpaşa and Kemer municipalities, and Lara Public Beach, operated by the Ministry of Culture and Tourism. The agenda covered beach management protocols, water quality monitoring, waste management systems, environmental education initiatives, and standards applied at Blue Flag marinas and tourist vessels.

Beyond site visits, the delegation studied how FEE Türkiye coordinates with public institutions, monitors bathing water quality, and maintains dialogue with tourism operators and civil society groups—insights considered essential for replicating the model at Issyk-Kul.

## A Potential First for Central Asia

Türkiye currently ranks third worldwide for the number of Blue Flag awards, according to Assoc. Prof. Dr. Abdulkadir Ateş, Chairman of FEE Türkiye's board. He noted that the country's accumulated expertise is increasingly serving as a reference for partner nations pursuing certification, and emphasized the significance of the Issyk-Kul process, which he said could become a milestone not only for Kyrgyzstan but for environmental and tourism cooperation across Turkic-speaking countries.

If the initiative advances successfully, sites around Issyk-Kul Lake could become the first in Central Asia to receive Blue Flag status, marking a significant development in the program's global expansion. The exchange is also seen as part of a broader effort to strengthen FEE's international network and extend sustainable tourism standards into new regions ahead of future certification cycles, including assessments anticipated in 2026.
